One of my least favorite programming pursuits is dealing with date formatting. Specifically figuring out the right format string to parse a given date. I think it's a complete waste of time, but I understand some environments have need to be very strict. I would say most people can take the risk of an incorrectly parsed date and just use a good date parser.

The best library I've ever used for parsing dates is python-dateutil for Python.  I hear Joda Time for Java is good but it seems to suffer from the "having to know your date format before you parse" problem.  Date parsing is hard, but python-dateutil does a great job for what I need it to so I can get on with my application.
