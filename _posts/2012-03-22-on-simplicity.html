One 140 character messaging application, one single-button touch screen iOS based smartphone, one side-scroller game with a red-clothes Italian plumber.

If you can look at your product and say that it embodies the escence of what your problem is, you have created something simple.  It can have many features, or just a few.  Like a fixed gear bike or or 21 speed road bike, based on the real-world requirements each is a simple solution to their respective task.

When trying to create something simple, strive not to create something feature-less, but rather the emodiment of the requirements. If your product has grown outside of the required bounds, it may have lost its simplicity.

What do you think? Is this how you view "simple" product development?
