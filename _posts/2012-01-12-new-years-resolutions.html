This year I've decided to write down some resolutions I'd like to achieve, along with ways I can keep improving myself.

Take More Risks

I'm disappointed that last year I did not make big changes in my life when I felt they were appropriate, instead dwelling on the "what-ifs?" and doing what was easy. I'm trying to learn how to build my software product development career, and I'm learning when to move on and when to stick it out. I have to be okay with doing things that scare my parents and leave me with gaps in employment. Most of the people I look up to have lead lives that deviate from societal norms. This is the year for big changes and to stop worrying about the "what-ifs?"

Express my Opinion More Often

This year I want to let more of my personality out. I don't want to worry about being liked by everyone. In private I'm not a neutral person, and I don't think I have to lose my personality to gain traction in my career and business pursuits.  As in product development, you never learn anything if you don't form an opinion and test it by getting it out to other people.

Be More Open to Cool Stuff

I almost missed out on Fallout 3 due to a misconception I had about the game.  Now it's one of my absolute favorites and I'm glad I gave it a shot.  This year I want to be more aware of false impressions.

Think About Food

Last year I tried going Vegan for several months.  It was one of the best things I've ever done.  I've gained staples that I can't live without, and I feel more enlightened about food, where it comes from, and the true costs of satisfying my conditioned American palate. This year I want to eat less meat, seafood, and dairy, and to support local farming by joining a CSA and spending more of my money at the farmer's market.

Think About Waste

I generate a large amount of waste just by maintaining a busy lifestyle.  This year I want to reduce my waste contributions. Driving less, eating fewer individually packaged foods, and conserving water are all possible ways to do this.

Write More Often

I express myself more clearly in writing than speech, and my friends and family often enjoy the things I write about.  This year I want to put more of my thoughts, ideas, dreams, opinions, and lessons learned into writing and to share them with the world. I want people to tell me I'm wrong and to help me become right. I want to share the things that have challenged my opinions and misconceptions, and hopefully help others challenge theirs.

Speak More

Public speaking is something I've always been terrified of, and I feel like I don't accurately project who I am when I speak in foreign situations. Right now the worst part for me is the part in between saying "yes" to an engagement, and the second leading up to the presentation.  I'm never nervous to say "yes," which is good because I should do it more this year.

Be a Better Boyfriend

My girlfriend and I live 90 miles apart.  This year I want to close that gap and spend more time with her.  She's more important to me than she realizes and I need to do a better job of showing that.
