--- layout: post title: Django Forms published: true meta: posterous_60e8ef2b7d9ddd23e14a941a8878b49c_post_id: "26197828" posterous_60e8ef2b7d9ddd23e14a941a8878b49c_permalink: http://maxlynchmadison.posterous.com/django-forms tags: [] type: post status: publish --- Lately I've noticed myself using Django Forms less and less often.  I do like being able to automatically validate and handle form errors, and I like the structure of creating a python class to represent a form.  However, I almost never use the django form output methods (as_p or as_table), and whenever I need to do something even the tiniest bit custom, I find myself having to dig through documentation and create custom form widgets.

I think it's just faster to do it by hand and have complete control over the form.
